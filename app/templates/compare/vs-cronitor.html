Duration monitoring — built in, not bolted on

Both DeadManCheck and Cronitor detect missing jobs. But DeadManCheck also alerts you when a job runs but takes longer than expected — automatically, based on its rolling average runtime.

Cronitor tracks execution logs per job, but doesn't automatically alert on duration anomalies without manual threshold configuration. DeadManCheck learns your job's normal runtime and flags deviations with no setup required.

Cronitor
Manual duration thresholds — you decide the limit, configure it per job, update it when job characteristics change.
DeadManCheck
Automatic baseline — learns your job's typical runtime, alerts when it drifts. Zero configuration needed.
