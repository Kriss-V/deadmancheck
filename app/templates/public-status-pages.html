Public Status Pages

Give your users a live view of your system health. Pick which monitors to display, publish a status page at a custom URL, and link it from your site — free on all plans.

How it works

Create a status page, choose a slug (e.g. status.yourcompany.com/s/your-slug), and select which of your monitors to display. The page updates in real time as monitors go up and down.

  • Custom slug — pick a memorable URL for your status page.
  • Choose your monitors — show only what's relevant to your users. Keep internal monitors private.
  • Live status — green/red indicators update as your monitors change state.
  • No login required — your users can view the page without an account.

Why a status page matters

When something goes down, your users notice before you do. A public status page gives them somewhere to check — reducing support tickets and building trust. It also signals that you take reliability seriously.

Cronitor charges $25/mo extra for status pages. DeadManCheck includes them on every plan, including free.

  • Cron job monitors — critical background jobs your users depend on
  • Data pipeline monitors — ETL jobs that feed your product's data
