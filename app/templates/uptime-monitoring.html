Website Uptime Monitoring

DeadManCheck pings your URLs on a schedule and alerts you the moment your site goes down — before your customers do. Response time tracking included on every check.

How it works

Add a URL, set a check interval, and choose your alert channels. DeadManCheck checks your site on schedule and alerts you immediately if it doesn't respond with the expected status code.

  • Check intervals — every 1, 5, 10, 15, 30, or 60 minutes.
  • Status code validation — alert if the response code doesn't match what you expect (default: 200).
  • Response time tracking — every check records latency in milliseconds. Spot slowdowns before they become outages.
  • Timeout detection — configurable timeout (5–30 seconds). If your site hangs, you get alerted.

Alert channels

Get notified wherever your team works. All channels are configured per monitor.

  • Email — instant down and recovery alerts
  • Slack — webhook-based alerts to any channel
  • Discord — webhook alerts for your server
  • Telegram — bot-based alerts to any chat
  • PagerDuty — full incident lifecycle (trigger + resolve)
  • Webhook — POST to any endpoint on your infrastructure

Performance metrics

Every uptime check records response time so you can track performance over time — not just availability. View check history per monitor to spot degradation before it causes an outage.

  • Response time per check (ms)
  • Full check history with timestamps
  • Down/recovery event log

Related features

  • Public status pages — share uptime with your users
  • Cron job monitoring — monitor scheduled jobs too
  • Output assertions — catch jobs that run but do nothing
